Jewish Heritage Tour

Explore the Rich Sephardic Jewish Heritage of Morocco

This tour takes you on a visit of the Melleh of Marrakech Jewish quarter, the Synagogue Salat Lazama built in 1492, and the Miara Jewish cemetery. The Lazama Synagogue, which was built by Jews fleeing Spain in 1492, is a beautiful example of the rich history of Moroccan Jews in Marrakech. The synagogue includes an interior courtyard — a popular Moroccan architectural design — and displays photographs and artifacts of Jewish life in Morocco. The Miara cemetery also dates back six hundred years, and it has tombstone from the 15th century when many Sephardic Jews relocated to Morocco from Andalusia.

After this we will visit the Bahia Palace. The Bahia Palace was built in the late 19th century, intended to be the greatest palace of its time. We will finish our tour with a full course Kosher lunch in a traditional Jewish restaurant followed by a walking tour of the artisan souks in the Medina.
